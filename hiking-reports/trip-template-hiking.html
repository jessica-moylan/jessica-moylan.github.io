Mount Marcy, New York

New York's Highest Peak • Adirondacks High Peaks

📅 August 15, 2025 📍 Keene Valley, NY ⛰️ Elevation: 5,344 ft

The Approach

Started from the Adirondack Loj at 5:00 AM to beat the crowds and catch sunrise from the summit. The Van Hoevenberg Trail is the most popular route to Marcy, and for good reason—it's well-maintained and offers a steady climb through beautiful forest before breaking above treeline.

The early morning light filtering through the trees made the first few miles particularly magical. We reached Marcy Dam around 6:15 AM as the sun was just starting to illuminate the surrounding peaks.

The Ascent

After Marcy Dam, the trail steepens considerably. The next 2.5 miles gain most of the elevation as you climb through dense forest. Around 4,000 feet, the trees start to thin out and you get your first glimpses of the higher peaks.

The final push to the summit is above treeline on exposed rock. This section can be tricky in wet conditions, but we had perfect weather—clear skies and light winds. The 360-degree views from the top are absolutely worth the effort.

The Summit

We reached the summit at 8:30 AM and had it almost to ourselves for about 20 minutes before other hikers started arriving. The views are incredible—you can see the Great Range, Algonquin, the McIntyre Range, and on a clear day like this, even the Green Mountains of Vermont.

The summit is marked by a USGS benchmark and a large pile of rocks. We spent about 45 minutes on top, eating breakfast and taking in the views. The temperature was perfect—cool but not cold, with just a light breeze.

The Descent

We took the same route down, which took about 3.5 hours with stops for photos and lunch. The descent is tough on the knees with all the rock steps, so trekking poles were definitely appreciated.

By the time we got back to the trailhead around 1:00 PM, the parking lot was completely full and there was a line of cars waiting. Starting early was definitely the right call.

Lessons Learned

  • Start early: We had the trail and summit mostly to ourselves until mid-morning
  • Bring layers: It's significantly cooler and windier above treeline
  • Trekking poles: Really helpful for the steep, rocky sections
  • Water: Bring plenty—there are limited opportunities to filter water on this route
  • Bug spray: The black flies were intense below 3,000 feet in August

Route Details

Trailhead: Adirondack Loj (High Peaks Information Center)
Route: Van Hoevenberg Trail out-and-back
Permits: None required (day use)
Parking: $15 day use fee, lot fills up by 7 AM on weekends
Season: Best from June through October
